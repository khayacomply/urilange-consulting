Company Registration

Standard CIPC company registration typically takes 1-5 business days, depending on CIPC's processing times. We submit your application electronically for the fastest possible turnaround. Complex registrations or name reservations may take slightly longer.

To register a Pty Ltd company, you'll need:
  • Certified copy of your South African ID (or passport for foreign directors)
  • Proof of residential address (not older than 3 months)
  • Proposed company name (up to 4 options)
  • Details of all directors and shareholders
  • Business address (can be residential)
  • Estimated annual turnover
Contact us for a complete checklist specific to your needs.

A Pty Ltd (Private Company) is the most common business structure in South Africa. It offers limited liability protection, meaning your personal assets are protected if the business faces debts. Other types include:
  • NPC (Non-Profit Company): For charitable organizations
  • Personal Liability Company: For professionals like lawyers and accountants
  • State-Owned Company: For government entities
We'll help you choose the right structure for your business.

Yes! Foreign nationals can register companies in South Africa. You'll need a valid passport, proof of address (which can be from your home country), and in some cases, a valid visa or work permit. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Tax & Compliance

A Tax Clearance PIN is a certificate from SARS confirming your tax compliance status. You need it for:
  • Applying for government tenders
  • Obtaining a B-BBEE certificate
  • Import/export licenses
  • Certain business licenses
  • Financial institution requirements
We handle the entire application process with SARS on your behalf.

VAT registration is mandatory when your business's taxable turnover exceeds R1 Million in any 12-month period. You can also voluntarily register if:
  • Your turnover has exceeded R50,000 in the past 12 months
  • You have a contractual obligation requiring VAT registration
  • You're making capital goods purchases exceeding R100,000
We'll assess your situation and advise on the best approach.

UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund): Mandatory if you employ anyone (including yourself as a director). It provides benefits to employees who lose their jobs.

COIDA (Compensation for Occupational Injuries and Diseases Act): Mandatory for all employers. It covers employees who get injured or contract diseases at work.

Both are legal requirements, and we handle the complete registration process for you.

CIPC requires all companies to file Annual Returns once every year, within 30 days of your company's anniversary date (the date it was registered). Failure to file can result in penalties or even deregistration of your company. We offer annual return filing services to keep you compliant.

B-BBEE & Tenders

B-BBEE (Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment) is a South African policy aimed at redressing the inequalities of apartheid by empowering previously disadvantaged groups. A B-BBEE certificate or affidavit:
  • Is required for government tenders
  • Helps you do business with large corporations
  • Improves your competitiveness in the market
  • Demonstrates your commitment to transformation
We help you obtain the right B-BBEE documentation for your business.

B-BBEE Affidavit: For Exempted Micro Enterprises (EMEs) with annual turnover below R10 million. It's a sworn statement declaring your B-BBEE level - quick and affordable.

B-BBEE Certificate: For Qualifying Small Enterprises (QSEs) with turnover between R10-R50 million, or larger businesses. Requires a formal verification by an accredited agency - more detailed and comprehensive.

The Central Supplier Database (CSD) is a mandatory database for any business wanting to do work with any government organ of state (national, provincial, or local). Registration is free but must be done correctly. We handle your CSD registration to ensure you're ready for government opportunities.

The Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB) registration is mandatory for any business wanting to undertake construction work for public sector clients. You'll receive a grading (1-9) based on your financial capacity and experience. We help you register and get the appropriate grading for your business.
